Fees & methodology
why net ≠ gross
All values shown in AUD. Stocks priced in USD by Alpaca and converted at the live AUD/USD rate (sourced from the European Central Bank via Frankfurter); the entry price stored uses the FX rate at the moment of purchase, so AUD P&L includes any FX movement.

Gross P&L is the market move only — current value minus capital deployed, before any transaction costs.
Net P&L subtracts an estimated round-trip fee for closing the position right now.

CoinSpot (crypto): The bot uses Instant Buy/Sell, which is 1% per side = 2% round-trip. There is also a 1–2% bid/ask spread baked into the quote — not separately shown. Market orders would be 0.1% per side, but the bot does not use those.
Alpaca (US stocks): $0 commission on buys; ~0.05% SEC/TAF on sells. The real cost for an Aussie is the AUD↔USD conversion to fund/withdraw — about 0.5% per direction via Wise, so ~1% round-trip. This is paid outside Alpaca but is included in the net figure.

Paper mode charges no actual fees. The numbers above show what real fees would be in live mode, so the net P&L is a realistic forecast of what you'd pocket on close.

How it works
Evening briefing — 8pm daily. Uses Claude Sonnet 4.5. Costs about 5 cents per run.
Crypto check — every 15 min, 24/7. Uses Claude Haiku (cheaper). Often skipped entirely when there's nothing to decide.
Intraday check — every 5 min during US market hours. Uses Haiku. Mechanical stops fire without calling Claude at all.
Q&A — your dashboard questions. Uses Sonnet. About 1.2 cents per question.

Hard cap: $2 USD per day. If the bot hits that, it stops calling Claude for the rest of the day (positions still get managed mechanically). Typical month: $15-30 USD.
